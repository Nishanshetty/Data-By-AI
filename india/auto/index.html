An Economic Engine Fuelling National Growth

Contribution to India's GDP (FY2024)

The automotive industry contributes a staggering 7.1% to India's total GDP and an immense 49% to its manufacturing GDP, highlighting its central role in the nation's industrial and economic health.

Global Production Powerhouse

India's dominance is multifaceted, leading globally in 3-wheeler manufacturing and ranking in the top tier for 2-wheelers, passenger vehicles, and commercial vehicles. This diverse production base provides resilience and a strong foundation for exports.

Vision 2030: The Road Ahead

The industry is guided by an ambitious roadmap to significantly increase its global footprint, with a strategic focus on becoming a high-value player in the global auto component trade.

A Leap in Global Component Trade

While a major vehicle producer, India's share of the global auto component trade is a modest 3%. Vision 2030 aims to nearly triple this to 8%, a move from an assembly hub to an integrated, high-value manufacturing power.

Strategic Goal:

Achieve a $25 Billion trade surplus in auto components by 2030, marking a new era of export-led growth.

Market Dynamics: A Diverse and Growing Landscape

Passenger Vehicles

4.3M units sold (FY25)

Record sales driven by a major shift towards premium Utility Vehicles (UVs), which now constitute 65% of sales, indicating evolving consumer preferences for features and technology.

Commercial Vehicles

~1M units sold (FY25)

Growth is anchored by massive government spending on infrastructure and the boom in e-commerce, which fuels demand for last-mile delivery vehicles.

Two-Wheelers

20.5M units sold (2024)

This cornerstone of Indian mobility is undergoing a dual disruption from electrification and a consumer shift towards more fuel-efficient models due to rising fuel costs.

Three-Wheelers

87% YoY Growth (2023)

Exhibiting explosive growth, this segment holds immense potential for rapid electrification due to its role in public transport and lower operating costs for EVs.

Passenger Vehicle Market Share (2024)

The market is highly concentrated, with the top four manufacturers—Maruti Suzuki, Hyundai, Tata Motors, and Mahindra—commanding a combined 80% of the market share, indicating intense competition at the top.

The Electric Vehicle Revolution

Explosive Growth in EV Adoption

India's EV sector is on an exponential growth path. Favorable policies, rising consumer awareness, and a narrowing price gap with ICE vehicles are accelerating this transition, positioning India to become a leading EV market by 2030.

Key Drivers

  • Strong Government Policies (FAME, PLI)
  • Growing Consumer Awareness of Cost Savings
  • Expanding Charging Infrastructure
  • Corporate Fleet Adoption

Persistent Challenges

  • High Upfront Vehicle Costs (Battery)
  • "Range Anxiety" & Inadequate Charging Infra
  • Reliance on Imported Components
  • Critical Skill Gaps in EV Technology

A Global Export Hub in the Making

Capitalizing on the "China Plus One" Strategy

As global firms diversify supply chains, India presents a "once-in-a-generation opportunity." With competitive labor costs, strong policy support, and growing capabilities, India is an increasingly attractive destination for manufacturing and exports.

Indian auto components are exported globally, with North America and Europe being the largest markets. This diversified export profile reduces risk and opens doors for further expansion under new free trade agreements.
