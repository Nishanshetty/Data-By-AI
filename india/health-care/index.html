The Dual System: Public vs. Private Infrastructure

India's healthcare delivery is characterized by a two-speed system. The public sector aims for broad, affordable access, while the private sector drives capacity and technology, commanding a majority of the nation's critical healthcare infrastructure. This chart visualizes the stark quantitative dominance of the private sector.

The Healthcare Delivery Structure

The system is organized in a tiered structure to manage patient flow from basic local care to highly specialized treatments. This model is designed to provide comprehensive care, starting from community-level health promotion to advanced surgical interventions in metropolitan centers.

Primary Care

Sub-Centres (SCs) & Primary Health Centers (PHCs). First point of contact for basic health, immunization, and maternal care.

→

Secondary Care

Community Health Centers (CHCs) & District Hospitals. Referrals from primary care for more specialized services.

→

Tertiary Care

Super-Specialty & Teaching Hospitals. Highest level for complex procedures like cancer treatment and organ transplants.

The Shifting Funding Landscape

Government spending on health is steadily rising, leading to a welcome decline in the burden of out-of-pocket payments by citizens. This trend signifies a crucial move towards greater financial protection and universal health coverage, though the journey is far from over.

Out-of-Pocket Burden

While decreasing, India's out-of-pocket expenditure remains significantly higher than the global average and that of many major economies, highlighting a persistent challenge in healthcare affordability for the average family.

The Great Divide: Urban vs. Rural Access

One of the most profound challenges is the geographical disparity in healthcare access. Quality care is heavily concentrated in cities, leaving the majority of the population in rural areas underserved, with inadequate facilities and a critical shortage of medical professionals.

60% vs 37%
